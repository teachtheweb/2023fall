Net Art: Online Contemporary Art Practices
Spring 2024
07:080:301:90
Rutgers Arts Online, Rutgers University
Syllabus

Since the birth of the World Wide Web in 1989, artists have contended with the rise of networked culture. In this online asynchronous course, students explored the history of internet art—from the early hacktivist net.art of the 1990s to contemporary works that engage with NFTs and artificial intelligence. Throughout the semester, students developed an understanding of the internet as a medium for and subject of creative expression—where did it come from, how does it work, how do we make things for it, and what do we want to say? Class discussions considered the cultural, social, political, and philosophical implications of the internet, as well as the creative responses of artists, designers, and technologists. Throughout the course, students completed their own web-based creative projects.
